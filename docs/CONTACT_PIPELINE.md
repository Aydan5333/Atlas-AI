# TSC Contact + Capability Pipeline

## Purpose

This document tracks the circles, people, organizations, skills, resources, and relationship paths TSC should study, follow, contact, and eventually collaborate with.

The rule is simple:

> Do not reach out empty-handed. Build proof, then ask for direction.

Atlas should eventually turn this document into a living contact intelligence system. Contacts should not be treated like a flat address book. Each contact should help Atlas understand the real-world network around TSC: skills, abilities, tools, spaces, interests, constraints, projects, and possible collaboration paths.

## Core idea

Every contact should answer:

- Who are they?
- What world or pillar are they connected to?
- What can they do?
- What tools, spaces, resources, or knowledge do they have?
- What projects could they support or advise?
- What proof should TSC build before approaching them?
- What would be respectful, useful, and realistic to ask?
- What other contacts or projects could they naturally connect with?

Atlas should learn from every contact and every project, then look for ways to bring the network together without forcing it.

## Contact stages

| Stage | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Study | Follow, research, learn the language, collect examples |
| Map | Record skills, resources, circles, and possible project fit |
| Prepare | Build one-pager, deck, proof reel, or relevant question |
| Warm Contact | Use existing relationship or mutual context |
| Direction Ask | Ask for advice, not a handout |
| Collaboration Ask | Ask for a specific small collaboration after proof exists |
| Sponsor Ask | Ask for product, money, repost, access, or support after deliverables are clear |
| Active Project | Contact is connected to an actual project, task, or proof lane |
| Follow-Up | Relationship is maintained with notes, value created, and next steps |

## Project planning around contacts

Each project should connect to contacts by capability.

Example project questions:

- Who has the skill to help this?
- Who has the space or tools?
- Who can teach this?
- Who can document this?
- Who can make the product better?
- Who can help make it safer?
- Who should only be contacted after proof exists?
- What value does TSC bring before asking anything?

## Atlas synthesis rule

Atlas should not only store contacts. Atlas should notice patterns.

Examples:

- A car audio builder plus a photographer plus a fashion sample can become a clean build recap.
- A mural artist plus a garment printer plus a music producer can become a drop campaign.
- A CAD mentor plus a sim racing setup plus an FSAE contact can become an Academy vehicle dynamics workshop.
- A local venue plus a DJ plus a safe car feature can become a small Culture Garage proof night.
- A business advisor plus a grant deadline plus a property lead can become a funding prep sprint.

The goal is not to force collaborations. The goal is to see the network clearly enough to create respectful opportunities.

## First outreach scripts

### ON 1 / event production direction ask

I saw your work on Hijacked: A Tokyo Bay Takeover and it connected directly with what I am building in Albuquerque through TSC, The Street Collective. We are building a smaller culture garage concept around cars, music, fashion, live art, sim racing, and AI event operations. I am not reaching out asking for a handout. I am trying to learn how to build this the right way. Would you be open to pointing me toward what a small brand needs to prove before being taken seriously for larger cultural activations?

### CarService / Clutch Kickback direction ask

Your car runway at the Tokyo Bay Takeover was exactly the kind of bridge between car culture and street culture that I have been working toward with TSC in New Mexico. I am building a local proof of concept with show cars, audio, art, fashion, and sim racing. I would appreciate any advice on how you structure car selection, safety, and storytelling for an activation like that.

### CACT.US Design Center / TXRX Labs direction ask

I am a mechanical engineering student and founder of TSC, a small Albuquerque based culture brand connecting automotive design, fashion, music, art, and youth skill building. I saw the work being done through CACT.US Design Center and TXRX Labs, especially around design education, fabrication, sneakers, and engineering. I would love to learn whether there are workshops, visits, volunteer paths, or ways to connect with the design education side.

### UNM FSAE first contact

Hello, my name is Aydan Holguin. I am currently a mechanical engineering student at CNM with plans to transfer to UNM, and I am very interested in the LOBOmotorsports Formula SAE program.

I am working toward building skills in automotive engineering, fabrication, CAD, and motorsports, and I would love to tour the facility or learn what I should focus on before transferring.

I also run a small local project called TSC, The Street Collective, which connects automotive culture, fashion, music, art, and technology. Long-term, I would love to help create community-facing content or educational events that make engineering more visible and exciting to students in New Mexico.

For now, I would mainly appreciate the chance to learn, observe, and understand how I could become useful to the team.

Thank you for your time,
Aydan Holguin

## Safety rules

- Do not use school, university, brand, or sponsor logos without permission.
- Do not imply official partnerships until written approval exists.
- Do not contact high-level culture accounts with a weak ask.
- Do not send protected Atlas information to sponsors.
- Do not let call agents contact people without approved scripts and human permission.
- Do not reduce a contact to what TSC can get from them. Track what value TSC can create for them too.
